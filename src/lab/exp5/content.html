Soil Mechanics Lab
Core Cutter Experiment
Introduction

This experiment was adopted by the Indian standard institution on September 22 1975 in IS 2720 which deals with the methods of tests for the soils.
In this we have a cylindrical core cutter of 130mm long and 10cm internal diameter with a wall thickness of 3mm hevelled at one end, The cutter shall be kept properly greased or oiled.

Note

  1. Length of Cutter - If the average density over a smaller depth is required then the appropriate length of cutter should be used.
  2. Where situations permit, for quality control cutters have also been used.

Theory

The method can be used successfully whenever soil conditions permit pushing of cutter for sampling and taking it out in the laboratory without much disturbance.

Unit weight is designed as the weight per unit volume. Here the weight and volume of soil comprise the whole soil mass. The voids in the soil may be filled with both water and air or only air or only water consequently the soil may be wet, dry or saturated. In soils the weight of air is considered negligible and therefore the saturated unit weight is maximum, dry unit weight is minimum and wet unit weight is in between the two. If soils are below water table, submerged unit weight is also estimated.

Unit weight of soil reflects the strength of soil against compression and shear. Unit weight of soil is used in calculating the stresses in the soil due to its overburden pressure. It is the unit weight of the soil which controls the field compaction and it helps in the design of embankment slopes. Permeability of soil depends on its unit weight .It may be noted here that , in the field the unit weight refers to dry unit weight only because the wet unit weight of soil at location varies from season to season and based on the fluctuations of the local water table level and surface water.

Procedure

  1. Calculate internal volume of core cutter.
  2. Measure weight of the core cutter in grams.
  3. Level 30 cm2 soil layer to be tested.
  4. Place the cutter on soil layer and steel dolly on the cutter.
  5. The steel dolly should be rammed down vertically.
  6. Then dig the soil surrounding the cutter and remove the cutter.
  7. Flatten the soil core at the ends of cutter with straight edge.
  8. Weigh the cutter along with the soil core.
  9. Collect a sample from the soil core to determine water content.
