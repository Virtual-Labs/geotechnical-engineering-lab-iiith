Theory

The California Bearing Ratio(CBR) test was developed by the California Division of Highways as a method of classifying and evaluating soil sub-grade and base course materials for flexible pavements. CBR is a measure of resistance of a material to penetration of standard plunger under controlled density and moisture conditions. CBR test may be conducted in re-molded or undisturbed sample. Test consists of causing a cylindrical plunger of 50mm diameter to penetrate a pavement component material at 1.25mm/minute. The loads for 2.5mm and 5mm are recorded. This load is expressed as a percentage of standard load value at a respective deformation level to obtain CBR value.

It is the ratio of force per unit area required to penetrate a soil mass with standard circular piston at the rate of 1.25 mm/min. to that required for the corresponding penetration of a standard material.
C.B.R. = { Test load / Standard load }x 100

Procedure

  1. Take the mold and put surcharge weights on the mold.
  2. Place the mold assembly on penetration testing machine.
  3. Place the penetration piston at the center of specimen with less load.
  4. Set stress and strain dial gauge reading to zero.
  5. Apply load on piston with penetration rate of 1.25 mm/min.
  6. Record the load at 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 2.5, 3.0, 4.0, 5.0, 7.5, 10 and 12.5 mm penetrations.
  7. Note maximum load & corresponding penetration.
  8. Remove the mold from loading machine.
  9. Take 20 - 50 gm of soil from 3 cm layer at top.
  10. Determine moisture content in the above sample.
